I.The input
problem

A Counsel is only as good as what it reads.

Point it at a folder of stale notes and it deliberates on stale notes. The intelligence depends on the input lane — and the input lane was the gap.

Your day is scattered: a thought jotted here, a meeting on the calendar, a task deferred again, an article saved to read later, a note in Apple Notes you'll never find again. None of it lands in one place your Counsel can read.

A docket is the list of matters brought before a council. Docket is a small, local, frictionless place where you keep your notes — the ones you write and the ones you drop in — that doubles as the lane feeding your Counsel.

All eight principles →

What it does

Capture, distilled, handed off.

i.

Capture

Daily notes, quick thoughts, tasks and checkboxes — in plain Markdown, plain files on the disk you chose. Click a day, get a templated note.

ii.

Reschedule, don't lose

An unfinished * [ ] task moves to another day with its history intact. The deferral itself becomes signal your Counsel can read.

iii.

Aggregate

Connectors pull scattered local context — calendar, mail, Apple Notes, feeds — into the day's note as readable, summarised Markdown. Never a raw dump.

iv.

Organise the way you work

A folder hierarchy that follows PARA — Projects, Areas, Resources, Archives. No database, no graph view. Just folders of files you own.

v.

Hand off to Counsel

Inner Counsel reads the same folder and deliberates. Its briefings return into a read-only Counsel/ section — findings where you already work.

vi.

Yours to keep

Stop using Docket and lose nothing — the notes are plain Markdown on your disk. The app is a lens over your files, not a silo that traps them.

The connector layer

Three tiers, and the boundary is a decision, not a limit.

Local Free
On your Mac, or apps macOS already authenticates. No OAuth, nothing leaves the machine.
Calendar · Reminders · Mail (on-device summary) · Apple Notes · Safari Reading List
"distill, not firehose"
Personal Free
Your own off-device sources, pulled with no stored credentials — a URL you pasted, fetched https-only.
ICS calendar feeds · RSS / Atom feeds
"keyless by design"
Corporate Outer Counsel · Paid
Keyed, OAuth, employer-owned data. Deliberately not built into Docket — these belong to the paid Outer Counsel tier.
Jira · Confluence · Slack · OneNote
"no credentials are stored in Docket"

No shared code, no IPC, no database. Either app works completely without the other. Data flows one way at a time and never loops — the only cross-app write is a single config pointer, never note data.
