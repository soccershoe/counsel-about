Corporate Counsel

Counsel at the scale of a team, a department, or a whole organisation — and the home for everything the free tier deliberately won't touch.

In development · Paid tier
§Why it's
a separate product

The boundary is a decision, not a limit.

Inner Counsel and Docket are free because they work for you, on your machine, with nothing to leak. The moment a connector needs a stored credential, an OAuth grant, or touches employer-owned data, it leaves the personal tier — by design.

Those keyed, corporate sources don't belong in a free local app. They need an admin, a credential store, and a clear owner. So they live here, in Corporate Counsel — the paid organisational tier that absorbs what was scoped as Outer and Grand Counsel into one product.

The principle that governs the free tier doesn't move: no credentials in Docket, nothing leaves the personal machine unasked. Corporate Counsel adds capability above that line — it never erases it.

Enforced, not promised — Principle viii in the paid tier

The two principles that protect the individual are structural.

Principle iv · The publish boundary is sacred

Data flows up only

Team features publish only the sanitised summaries an individual explicitly chooses — never raw notes, personal context, window titles, file paths, or capture data. The team tier sees what its members chose to share; it never looks down into individual data.

Enforced by silos.py — sanitisation strips raw data in code, so it cannot leak
Principle v · No comparison

Never a leaderboard

The team tier surfaces collective blind spots, shared momentum, and team-level risk — never individual rankings. Team Counselors answer team-level questions: where are we stuck, what are we collectively not seeing? They never answer questions about a person.

Enforced by the data model — individual-level queries are structurally impossible
