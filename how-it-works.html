How it all works

Capture, reflect, act — over one folder.

Four apps, no shared code, no server. You drop loops in, a plain-Markdown vault holds them in trust, and two readers hand them back — one for reflection, one for action.

The shape of it

Everything flows through one folder.

Each app works completely on its own — the structured paths degrade to simply reading the files. And the loop closes: the briefings and the daily slate come back to you.

§The mental
model

Capture → reflect → act.

Docket captures your day and pulls scattered context — calendar, reminders, mail, notes, feeds — into the vault. Inner Counsel reads it and deliberates. Slate reads it and assembles your daily actions.

You drop loops in, the vault holds them in trust, and two readers hand them back: one for reflection (be better), one for action (do better). That's the whole family in one line.

I.The
contract

The folder of Markdown is the source of truth.

Every durable thing flows through plain files. There's no shared code, no IPC, no database between the apps — the vault on disk is the entire integration surface.

Docket ships a small read-only server, docket-mcp, that reads those same files and hands back typed structure — a parser-as-a-service, never a second source of truth. So no app needs another app's live server to function; the structured paths degrade gracefully to just reading the folder. Each app works completely without the others.

II.One
direction

The loop never feeds back.

What an app writes never returns as if it were your input. Inner Counsel's briefings live in a Counsel/ folder it excludes from its own index. Slate's memory of what keeps slipping lives outside the vault entirely.

Data flows one way at a time — from the world, into the vault, out to the readers, back to you — and never circles. The boundary isn't a promise; it's the architecture.

III.All
local

Nothing leaves the machine.

Even the AI is local — the only network calls are to a model on localhost. No accounts, no telemetry, no server to phone home to.
