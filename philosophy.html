Eight rules · Architecturally enforced
Philosophy — the principles

A tool with
opinions, on
your behalf.

These principles are not marketing. They are constraints that govern every design decision in Counsel — enforced by architecture, not by good intentions.

Excerpted and adapted from the project's
internal PRINCIPLES.md, drafted March 2026.

§

Most workplace AI is built downward. It watches the worker and reports up — to the manager, to the platform, to the company. Counsel inverts that. It watches the worker and reports to the worker. Nothing leaves their machine unless they say so. Nothing about them is measured for anyone else's benefit. The architecture is the policy.

I.

Your Counsel works for you.

SOVEREIGNTY

Counsel serves one master: the person it's installed for. Not their manager. Not their employer. Not their department. Not the team that built it. Every Counselor, every briefing, every deliberation exists to help one person see their work more clearly, perform better, and understand their own value more completely.

When that principle is in tension with any other goal, the person wins.

II.

The employee already has the intelligence.

REFLECTION, NOT EXTRACTION

The surveillance model treats employees as resources to be measured and optimised from above. Counsel rejects that premise entirely. Knowledge workers accumulate enormous intelligence — domain expertise, pattern recognition, institutional memory, hard-won judgment. Most of it is invisible, even to themselves, because there is no system that helps them see it.

Counsel surfaces that intelligence. It does not extract value upward. It reflects value back to the person who created it.

III.

Data belongs to the session, not the surveillance system.

LOCAL FIRST

Counsel captures work context — note activity, window titles, patterns over time — for one purpose: to help you think more clearly about your own work. That data is never used to measure, rank, compare, or report on you to anyone else. On a personal install you own it completely. You can export, delete, and manage it on your own terms.

IV.

The publish boundary is sacred.

ONE-WAY VALVE

When team features are eventually enabled, Counsel can publish sanitised summaries to a shared team silo. This is opt-in, always explicit, always controlled by the individual. Raw notes are never published. Personal context is never published. Window titles, file paths, and capture data are never published. Per-Counselor toggles let you share strategic thinking without sharing wellness signals — or share wins without sharing troubleshooting notes.

Data flows up only. The team tier sees aggregate patterns. It never looks down.

V.

Counsel never compares employees to each other.

NO LEADERBOARD

The team tier surfaces collective blind spots, shared momentum, and team-level risks. It does not rank individuals. It does not show who publishes more. It does not surface whose Brag Documenter entries are longer or whose Counselors flagged more risks. The moment Counsel becomes a leaderboard, the principle is broken. If users fear comparative evaluation, they will sanitise their inputs — and sanitised inputs produce degraded deliberation. The no-comparison rule protects the integrity of the system itself.

VI.

Opt-in is the only mode.

CONSENT BY DEFAULT

Every feature that increases what Counsel observes or shares requires explicit user action. Note & window-title capture is the default. Meeting capture is opt-in. Publishing is off by default. The Wellness Counselor is disabled by default. No feature silently begins observing more. No update changes a sharing setting. You are never surprised by what Counsel knows or shares.

VII.

Inferences are labelled honestly.

EPISTEMIC HUMILITY

Counsel draws inferences from context — stress signals from work patterns, career trajectory from task types, team dynamics from communication. These are useful. They are also uncertain. Every inference is labelled as what it is — a pattern observed from limited data, not a diagnosis or a fact. The Wellness Counselor does not tell you that you are burned out. It says: “The pattern of your recent work suggests increasing load without corresponding recovery. You may want to pay attention to this.”

VIII.

The architecture enforces the principles.

CODE, NOT POLICY

These principles are not policy statements followed by good intentions. They are enforced by the architecture. Data sovereignty is enforced by local-first storage. The publish boundary is enforced by code that strips raw data before it can leak. Per-Counselor toggles are enforced by config, not UI convention. The no-comparison rule will be enforced by the team tier's data model — individual-level queries are structurally impossible, not merely prohibited.

If a principle can be violated by changing a setting, it is not yet enforced. The goal is to make violations architecturally impossible, not merely discouraged.

·

A tool on a managed endpoint operates within an organisational context. Counsel's job is still to serve the person, within whatever environment they're in.
