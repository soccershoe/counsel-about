How it works — the deliberation

Independent first.
Reactive second.
Synthesised last.

The architecture has not changed since V1. Independent analysis, structured reaction, one synthesis. Everything else is plumbing.

Engine — stdlib Python, zero pip. Backend — local LLM (Ollama) by default,
OpenAI-compatible or corporate endpoint optional.

Two rounds, one synthesis

A session in three movements.

I. ROUND ONE

Independent first takes.

Each Counselor reads the same context — your notes, your active windows, your personal context — and writes their analysis without seeing anyone else. The Librarian extracts evidence. The Auditor flags risk. The Strategist asks if the work builds leverage. Ten lenses, ten paragraphs. No groupthink yet.

  • InputsNotes · Window titles · Personal context
  • ConcurrencyOne LLM call per Counselor
  • MemoryEach Counselor receives their last three Round 1 outputs
II. ROUND TWO

Structured reaction.

Each Counselor reads every other Counselor's Round 1 output and reacts from their own lens. The Auditor pushes back on the Strategist's recommendation. The Diplomat flags that the Strategist's visibility play would alienate a key stakeholder. The Troubleshooter says, “you tried this in October and it didn't work.” Round 2 is where the disagreements live. The disagreements are the value.

  • InputsAll Round 1 outputs · cross-Counselor memory
  • ConcurrencyOne LLM call per Counselor
  • OutputConsidered position after debate
III. SYNTHESIS

The Orchestrator's closing.

A final pass weights Round 2 heavily — the considered position after debate is more meaningful than the first take. It surfaces the most interesting disagreements as a Key Tensions section, then writes a single paragraph synthesis you can read in under a minute. The full deliberation is preserved for the days you want to dig.

  • InputsAll Round 1 + Round 2 outputs
  • OutputBriefing markdown · saved to your notes folder
  • Total≈ 21 LLM calls · 3–8 min on Apple Silicon
Five clocks

Each cadence reads
different inputs.

Daily reads raw context. Everything longer reads the previous summaries. That's how the system sees arcs, not incidents.

Daily

TACTICAL
24h
Reads notes & active context. Asks: what happened, what to do tomorrow.

Weekly

RHYTHM
7d
Reads the week's daily summaries. Asks: what kept getting deferred? Where did energy actually go?

Monthly

PROGRESS
30d
Reads the month's daily summaries. Am I making progress on what matters, or only staying busy?

Quarterly

TRAJECTORY
90d
Reads the quarter's daily summaries. Career trajectory check. Wins for self-review. Skills developing vs. intended.

Yearly

ARC
365d
Reads the year's daily summaries. Who were you at the start of this year vs. now? What surprised you?
The shape of the engine

Small parts. One file each.

counsel.py

The deliberation engine. Two rounds, five cadences, one synthesis.

db.py

SQLite layer. Sessions, Counselor outputs, notes index, personal-context versions, feedback.

backend.py

AI backend abstraction. Local Ollama, personal cloud, or corporate endpoint — one config switch.

memory.py

Cross-Counselor memory. Each advisor remembers what they said and what their colleagues said.

skills.py

Counselor plugins. Drop a JSON file in ~/.counsel/counselors/ and it joins the table.

silos.py

Publish engine. The boundary between private and shared. Sanitises before anything leaves.

harvest.sh

Context harvester. Captures notes & window titles every 30 minutes via launchd.

mcp_server.py

MCP server. Exposes briefings & deliberation to other AI tools — Claude Desktop, Cursor.

Total — about 3,900 lines of Python and bash. Zero pip dependencies. The whole thing fits in a folder you can copy with one command.
