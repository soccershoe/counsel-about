Slate how-to

Point Slate at your vault, set when the morning slate arrives, and read your daily three. There's no list to maintain — that's the point.

I.Install &
connect

Slate reads through Docket.

Download Slate from the download page and open it. It reads your vault the structured way — through Docket's docket-mcp helper — so you'll want Docket installed with a vault open.

There's nothing to wire up by hand: Slate finds the vault the same way every reader in the suite does. How the vault connection works →

II.The
model

It borrows Inner Counsel's model.

With nothing to set, Slate follows the local model Inner Counsel is already configured to use — so the two apps share one resident model in memory instead of loading two.

Assembling the slate is a single focused local call, and the context never leaves your Mac: the endpoint is clamped to your machine unless you explicitly opt into a remote one.

III.The morning
slate

Pick a time; it pushes the day.

Set when you want the slate — a morning notification at a time you choose — and Slate hands you about three prioritised actions with one line of rationale each. It lives in the menu bar, with a real window you can Cmd-Tab to.

You read it and go. There's no inbox to process first.

IV.No
grooming

Stale things fade.

You never see a backlog. Items you keep not doing quietly stop surfacing rather than piling up, and things that keep slipping rise to the top, tagged with how long they've been open.

Pin something to keep it in view; otherwise, just trust the slate to bring back what matters. About Slate →
