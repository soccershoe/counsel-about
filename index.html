A private advisory panel for your working life

A council that knows your work, and reports only to you.

Counsel watches the context of your work and convenes a panel of advisors to deliberate on it — entirely on your own machine. What follows are the principles that govern it, and the three free products that live by them.

I.The premise
beneath it all

The employee already has the intelligence.

The surveillance model of workplace AI treats people as resources to be measured and optimised from above. Counsel rejects that premise entirely.

Knowledge workers accumulate enormous intelligence over years of work — domain expertise, pattern recognition, institutional memory, hard-won judgment. Most of it is invisible, even to themselves, because no system helps them see it.

Counsel surfaces that intelligence. It does not extract value upward; it reflects value back to the person who created it. The whole suite is an argument for that one inversion — and the principles below are the constraints that keep it honest.

The Counsel principles

Not marketing language — constraints that govern every decision.

i.

Your Counsel works for you

It serves one master: the person it's installed for — not their manager, their employer, their department, or the team that built it. When that's in tension with any other goal, the person wins.

ii.

The employee already has the intelligence

You are not made more valuable by being monitored. You are made more valuable by understanding yourself better. Counsel reflects your value back — it never extracts it upward.

iii.

Data belongs to the session, not the surveillance system

Work context is captured for one purpose: to help you think clearly about your own work. It is never used to measure, rank, compare, or report on you to anyone else.

iv.

The publish boundary is sacred

Team features publish only the sanitised summaries you explicitly choose — never raw notes, context, or capture data. Data flows up only; the team tier never looks down into individual data.

Holds in Corporate Counsel
v.

Counsel never compares employees to each other

The team tier surfaces collective blind spots and shared momentum — never a leaderboard. The moment Counsel ranks individuals, the principle is broken.

Holds in Corporate Counsel
vi.

Opt-in is the only mode

Every feature that widens what Counsel observes or shares requires explicit action. No feature silently begins observing more; no update changes a sharing setting.

vii.

Inferences are labeled honestly

Patterns drawn from limited data are named as exactly that — never a diagnosis or a fact. Counsel never claims to know more than it does.

viii.

The architecture enforces the principles

These are not promises kept by good intention. They are enforced in code — local-first storage, sanitisation that strips raw data, opt-in config defaults. A principle a checkbox can switch off isn't enforced.
