Inner Counsel how-to

Install the app, choose a model, point it at your vault, and run your first deliberation.

I.Install &
first run

Open it, and it sets itself up.

Download Inner Counsel from the download page, drag it to Applications, and open it. It lives in your menu bar and runs a small engine in the background — no account, no sign-in.

Everything it stores — sessions, briefings, your context — lives in ~/.counsel/ on your machine, fully exportable and deletable.

II.Choose a
model

Local by default; your call.

Out of the box Inner Counsel talks to a local model (Ollama) on your own machine — nothing leaves. If you'd rather, point it at an OpenAI-compatible endpoint or a corporate one; that's an explicit choice you make once, in Configuration.

Local keeps the whole deliberation private. A cloud model trades some of that privacy for quality — the trade-off is yours to make.

III.Point it at
your work

Your vault, and a little context.

In Configuration → Notes, set the notes folder to your Docket vault. Inner Counsel indexes it each run — prose only, skipping its own Counsel/ briefings so it never reads its own output.

Then write a short personal context — your role, goals, what you're trying to get better at — and enable the Counselors you want. The Wellness Counselor is off by default; turn it on only if you want it.

IV.Run &
read

Deliberate, then read the briefing.

Run a session and the panel deliberates across two rounds; the Orchestrator writes a one-paragraph synthesis with a Key Tensions section. Briefings land in your vault's Counsel/ folder — and show up read-only in Docket's sidebar.

Sessions run on five cadences, daily through yearly, so the picture builds over time. The roster →   How a session runs →
