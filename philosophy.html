Philosophy — the principles

A tool with opinions, on your behalf.

These are not marketing language. They are the constraints that govern every design decision in Counsel — enforced by architecture, not by good intentions.

§The inversion
beneath it all

Built upward, not downward.

Most workplace AI is built downward. It watches the worker and reports up — to the manager, to the platform, to the company. Counsel inverts that.

It watches the worker and reports to the worker. Nothing leaves their machine unless they say so. Nothing about them is measured for anyone else's benefit. The architecture is the policy — and the eight rules below are how that policy is kept.

The eight principles

Not marketing language — constraints that govern every decision.

i.

Your Counsel works for you

It serves one master: the person it's installed for — not their manager, employer, department, or the team that built it. When that's in tension with any other goal, the person wins.

Sovereignty
ii.

The employee already has the intelligence

The surveillance model measures people from above; Counsel rejects it. Knowledge workers already hold enormous intelligence, most of it invisible even to themselves. Counsel surfaces it and reflects value back — never extracts it upward.

Reflection, not extraction
iii.

Data belongs to the session

Work context is captured for one purpose: to help you think clearly about your own work. It is never used to measure, rank, compare, or report on you. On a personal install you own it completely — export, delete, manage on your terms.

Local first
iv.

The publish boundary is sacred

Team features publish only the sanitised summaries you explicitly choose — never raw notes, personal context, window titles, or capture data. Data flows up only; the team tier sees aggregate patterns and never looks down.

One-way valve
v.

Counsel never compares employees

The team tier surfaces collective blind spots and shared momentum — never individual rankings. The moment Counsel becomes a leaderboard the principle is broken; fear of comparison would degrade the very inputs it relies on.

No leaderboard
vi.

Opt-in is the only mode

Every feature that widens what Counsel observes or shares requires explicit action. Meeting capture, publishing, and the Wellness Counselor are all off by default. No update silently changes a sharing setting.

Consent by default
vii.

Inferences are labelled honestly

Patterns drawn from limited data are named as exactly that — never a diagnosis or a fact. The Wellness Counselor doesn't say you're burned out; it says the pattern suggests rising load without recovery, and you may want to look.

Epistemic humility
viii.

The architecture enforces the principles

These aren't promises kept by good intention — they're enforced in code: local-first storage, sanitisation that strips raw data, opt-in config defaults. If a setting can violate a principle, it isn't yet enforced.

Code, not policy
