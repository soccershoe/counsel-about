Vol. I — No. 05The Privacy Boundary
Local-first · Architecturally enforced
Privacy — the boundary

Nothing leaves
your machine.
Unless you say so.

Privacy is not a feature in Counsel. It is the architecture. The code physically cannot send your raw notes anywhere — because the code that would do it does not exist.

See Principle III and Principle VIII.
Architecture, not policy.

The two columns

What stays.
What can leave.

STAYS LOCAL · ALWAYS

On your machine, in your home folder.

  • ●
    Your notes. The full text of every Markdown, text, or PDF file Counsel reads.
  • ●
    Window titles & capture data. What apps you used, what files were open, when.
  • ●
    Personal context. Your goals, your role, your growth interests, your frustrations.
  • ●
    Briefing history. Every Round 1, Round 2, and Synthesis ever written, in SQLite.
  • ●
    The notes index. Embeddings and per-file summaries for semantic search.
  • ●
    Wellness signals. Inferred load, late-night patterns, deferred-item history.

Stored in ~/.counsel/. SQLite + plain text + Markdown. Fully exportable. Fully deletable. Yours.

CAN LEAVE · ONLY IF YOU CHOOSE

And only what you whitelist.

  • ○
    The Orchestrator's synthesis, if you turn publishing on.
  • ○
    Selected Counselor outputs — per-Counselor toggle, off by default.
  • ○
    Sanitised metadata — date, cadence, session id. No raw notes, no window titles, no paths.

Publishing is off by default. The publish engine (silos.py) strips raw data before anything is written to a publish package. Even then, you choose where it goes.

Three commitments

In the order they matter.

I.

No accounts. No telemetry. No analytics.

Counsel does not have a server. There is nowhere for telemetry to go. Installing the app does not register you anywhere. There is no “anonymous usage data” pipe. The application you install on your machine is the entire product.

II.

The publish boundary is in code, not policy.

silos.py is the only path data takes to leave the machine. It strips window titles, file paths, raw note content, and personal context before anything is written. Per-Counselor publish toggles are enforced by config, not by UI convention. If a feature could violate a principle by changing a setting, it is not yet enforced.

III.

The team tier never looks down.

When Counsel eventually grows team features, the team server only receives what individuals choose to publish. It cannot query into individual raw data — that path is structurally impossible, not merely prohibited. There is no leaderboard. No ranking. No comparing employees to each other. Ever.

An honest note

Caveats, not
marketing copy.

On a personal install — your laptop, your Mac, your home directory — Counsel is fully local and fully yours. On a managed corporate device, Counsel still works only for you, but device-level data may be subject to IT policies that exist outside the application. Counsel does not change what it does in those environments. It does not build features that make that surveillance easier.

If you choose a cloud LLM backend instead of the local default, your context is sent to that provider on each call — that is a tradeoff between local privacy and model quality, and the choice is explicitly yours, made once, in setup.

Inferences are inferences. The Wellness Counselor reads patterns from limited signal. It will not tell you that you are burned out. It will say, “the pattern of your recent work suggests increasing load without corresponding recovery.”

·

The goal is to make violations architecturally impossible,
not merely discouraged.

— Principle VIII
