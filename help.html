§Start here

Getting started — five minutes.

Pick a folder to be your vault — Docket creates it, or adopts a notes folder you already use. Inner Counsel and Slate read that same folder; you connect each one once, in any order. Connecting only adds the vault — it never moves or overwrites notes you already keep. Each app works on its own; together they share the one folder.

That's the whole setup — no accounts, no servers, nothing to sign up for. Get the apps →

§Where your
data lives

Everything stays on your Mac.

There's no cloud account and no server — each app reads or writes plain files on your own disk. Here's where, so you always know what to back up and what to delete.

The vault — your notes, in the plain folder you chose. You own it; back it up by copying it. Every other app is just a reader of this one folder.

Inner Counsel — keeps its sessions, briefings, and your personal context in ~/.counsel/.

Docket — writes your notes into the vault; its own settings live in ~/Library/Application Support/Docket/.

Slate — keeps its "what keeps slipping" memory in ~/Library/Application Support/Slate/, deliberately outside the vault so Inner Counsel never re-reads it.

No accounts, no servers, no telemetry — nothing leaves your machine unless you explicitly point an app at a remote model.
